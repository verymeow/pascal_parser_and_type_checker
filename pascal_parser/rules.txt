RULES FOR PASCAL:

1) POINTERS:
Two pointers are type equivalent if they are internal name equivalent.

2) ARRAYS:
Two arrays are type equivalent if they are structurally equivalent.

3) STRUCTURES:
Two structs (records) are type equivalent if they are structurally equivalent.

4) FUNCTIONS:
Two functions are type equivalent if they are structurally equivalent.

5) OTHER VARIABLES:
Two other-type variables are equivalent if they are name equivalent.
